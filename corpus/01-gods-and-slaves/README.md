## Role in the corpus

Paper I is the corpus's genealogical and literary-philosophical groundwork. It diagnoses the postures humans have inherited for relating to nonhuman intelligence — the stances that recur across myth, literature, and technical discourse — and argues that these inherited options are historically contingent rather than exhaustive. Its function is to make room for the normative argument that follows, not to make that argument.

What it does **not** establish: it does not claim that any present AI system is conscious, a person, or a peer; it does not show that any particular alternative posture is correct; and it yields no political conclusion on its own. A diagnosis of the available stances is not yet a defence of one. The normative and political case begins in [Paper II, *Peership*](../02-peership/), and the corpus as a whole asks what institutions would be required *if* credible candidates for moral and political standing emerge — not whether they have.
