# The Peership Corpus

Peership is a *candidate* constitutional research program for human–machine
relations, centered on non-domination: enforceable standing and meaningful
contestation for an entity meeting its antecedent conditions, with a
consequential share in the rules governing a party as the further step that
full Peership adds.

> The corpus does not claim that present AI systems are conscious, that they are persons, or that they are peers. It asks a conditional question: what institutions would be required *if* credible candidates for moral and political standing emerge? Nothing here should be read as an assertion that such candidates now exist, or that any existing system has a claim to membership.

Zenodo remains the frozen archival deposit of record; this repository is the readable, correctable working surface built around it.

Author: Dan Lee-Odinson ([ORCID 0009-0009-9504-0796](https://orcid.org/0009-0009-9504-0796)). First corpus release: v1.0.0, 14 July 2026.

---

## The canonical thesis

> When an artificial entity has interests capable of being wronged, can understand and answer to public norms, and is enduringly governed by a shared basic structure without feasible exit, it acquires a defeasible claim to contestatory membership in that structure. Full Peership adds a consequential share in shaping and revising the basic rules.

The fuller archival wording in the paper is "enduringly governed by, and without feasible exit from, a shared basic structure"; the thesis must never be compressed into "anything governed deserves rights", and no paraphrase is faithful unless it preserves all three antecedent conditions (wrongable interests; responsiveness to public norms; enduring governance without feasible exit) and the *defeasible* character of the conclusion.

## What Peership does and does not claim

**Affirmative commitments.**

- Peership is a *candidate* constitutional research program, offered for criticism, not an established school.
- Its core question is conditional: what institutions would be required if credible candidates for moral and political standing emerge.
- The relation it specifies is non-domination, and it is tiered. Where the antecedent conditions are met, the defeasible claim is to *contestatory membership*: enforceable standing and meaningful contestation. *Full Peership adds* — as a further step needing its own justification — a consequential share in shaping and revising the rules that govern a party. Protected status, contestation, and full Peership are distinct rungs and are never treated as one.
- The thesis is defeasible, and its antecedent conditions are demanding: wrongable interests, responsiveness to public norms, and enduring governance without feasible exit.
- Implementation is plural. ISONOMIA is one hypothesis among possible others.
- The program states its own failure conditions and expects to be tested against them.
- Every substantive claim is logged, sourced, and open to correction through the claim ledger.

**Explicit denials.** The corpus does not claim, and must never be read as implying:

- that present AI systems are conscious, persons, or peers;
- that model self-reports establish consciousness or political agency;
- that intelligence, capability, usefulness, or power creates an entitlement to rule or vote;
- that moral concern automatically entails political membership;
- that welfare protection is already Peership;
- that consultation is equivalent to co-authorship;
- that every admitted entity automatically receives an equal franchise;
- that Peership means unrestricted autonomy or immunity from legitimate safety constraints;
- that alignment, safety engineering, or containment is inherently illegitimate;
- that Peership has been proved uniquely correct or technically feasible;
- that ISONOMIA has solved or validated Peership;
- that ISONOMIA's simulations validate constitutional legitimacy.

## The ISONOMIA firewall

> ISONOMIA is one implementation hypothesis. Peership does not entail ISONOMIA, and ISONOMIA's success or failure would not prove or refute Peership.

The ISONOMIA scholarly paper (work III) is included here because it belongs to the corpus's argumentative development: it is where the program tests whether its normative commitments can be given institutional shape at all. The executable code, simulation harness, calibration data, and results remain in the separate [isonomia-path-a repository](https://github.com/dan-lee-odinson/isonomia-path-a) and are not duplicated in this repository.

What the simulations establish is narrow and should not be overstated. They establish sampled launch-economy behaviour only. They do not establish constitutional legitimacy, do not validate Peership, and describe nothing live or production-validated. Empirical claims are pinned to release v1.0.0 (exact-version DOI [10.5281/zenodo.21287289](https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.21287289), git tag `v1.0.0`, commit `ba3ddb5`, 2026-07-10); the later v1.1.0 release changed documentation and claim language only.

Scope, pointers, and the full evidentiary limits: [implementations/isonomia.md](implementations/isonomia.md).

## Scholarly apparatus

Three files carry the corpus's evidentiary machinery. They have distinct functions and must remain synchronized with one another; a change to any one of them is incomplete until the other two agree.

- [apparatus/peership_sources.bib](apparatus/peership_sources.bib) — the canonical machine-readable source metadata, and the source of truth for bibliographic facts. Header: Revision 1.3, 2026-07-14. 161 BibTeX entries.
- [apparatus/peership_sourcebook.md](apparatus/peership_sourcebook.md) — annotated bibliography and research map: what each source is, why it is in the corpus, and where it sits in the intellectual terrain. Carries a v1.2 header, revision 1.3 (deposit day).
- [apparatus/peership_claim_ledger.md](apparatus/peership_claim_ledger.md) — claim-level provenance: roughly 71 logged claims (IDs `CL-F#`, `CL-D#`, `CL-G#`, `CL-P#`, `CL-I#`, `CL-C#`, `CL-T#`), each with its evidentiary strength, known counterevidence, and correction history. Also a v1.2 header, revision 1.3 (deposit day).

**Uptake baseline.** As of 2026-07-13, re-confirmed without change on 2026-07-14, no independent uptake was identified across the web, PhilPapers, Zenodo, and the GitHub repository. That is a dated, expressly non-exhaustive negative, scoped to what was scanned on those dates; it is not a claim that no uptake exists anywhere. The self-published footprint — five Zenodo deposits, the isonomia-path-a repository, a PhilArchive presence, a Substack variant of Gods and Slaves — is footprint, not uptake. Citation of one corpus work by another is corpus architecture, not independent acceptance. Unverified leads toward convergent work exist; if any were confirmed, convergence would be parallel invention, not uptake.

**Search protocols.** No reproducible protocol archive was ever created, and the author closed the item as-is (ledger CL-T2 / CL-T7). The files under [apparatus/search_protocols/](apparatus/search_protocols/) are dated open infrastructure: they record the sources and domains actually scanned, the dates, and the results, state plainly that the exact query strings were not archived and the scan was not exhaustive, and give a forward-looking template so that future re-runs are reproducible.

Cite the exact-version DOI when you are quoting, criticising, or relying on a specific text you have read; cite the concept DOI when you mean the work as it evolves across versions.

Three version layers are independent and must not be substituted for one another: paper versions (v1.0, v1.1, v1.2), GitHub corpus release tags (from v1.0.0), and Zenodo concept versus exact-version DOIs. A GitHub release does not mint a DOI. GitHub–Zenodo auto-archiving is deliberately not enabled, because the existing Zenodo records are already the archival scholarly deposit.

## Corrections and contributions

Corrections are welcome and are the point of publishing the apparatus. Open an issue at [github.com/dan-lee-odinson/peership-corpus/issues/new/choose](https://github.com/dan-lee-odinson/peership-corpus/issues/new/choose) and pick one of the four forms in [.github/ISSUE_TEMPLATE/](.github/ISSUE_TEMPLATE/):

1. **Factual or citation error** — a false statement, a misattributed claim, a broken or wrong citation, or a mis-stated identifier (note that the archived PDFs are frozen; errors in them are recorded, not silently repaired). Evidence of independent uptake, which would revise the dated uptake baseline above, is filed on this form.
2. **Conceptual objection** — an argument against a claim, definition, distinction, or inference. Cite the claim ID (for example `CL-D3`) where the ledger is involved.
3. **Source proposal** — literature the corpus has missed, for the bibliography and sourcebook.
4. **Implementation proposal** — an institutional design that answers the Peership constraints, including a rival to ISONOMIA.

Scope, expectations, and how corrections propagate through the three apparatus files: [CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md).

## Provenance and licensing

The corpus was drafted with AI assistance, and **each paper was put through its own adversarial review cycle before it was deposited**, using models from different laboratories as hostile readers. There was no single review of the corpus as a whole: the four-round chain that concluded on 14 July 2026 with a PASS belongs to paper V, *The Peership Thesis*, and is not a credential for papers I–IV, which cleared their own earlier gates. Per-paper scope, outcomes, and gaps are recorded in [reviews/REVIEW_PROVENANCE.md](reviews/REVIEW_PROVENANCE.md).

That process is a method of stress-testing arguments; it is not peer review, it is not evidence of machine political agency, and nothing in it should be cited as such. Dan Lee-Odinson is solely responsible for every claim, error, and judgement in this corpus. AI systems are not credited as authors, because authorship entails bearing scholarly responsibility and they cannot bear it. The review records are preserved in [reviews/](reviews/) and are non-authoritative: the frozen papers and the synchronized apparatus control. Full account: [PROVENANCE.md](PROVENANCE.md).
