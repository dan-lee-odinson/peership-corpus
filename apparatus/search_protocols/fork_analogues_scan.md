## What was being looked for

An implemented analogue of **executable, unconfiscated fork**: a right of exit that (a) can actually be exercised — not merely declared — and (b) **takes the exiting party's assets with it**. The second condition is the demanding one. Exit that strips the departing party of everything it holds is not exit in the relevant sense; it is expulsion, or surrender. A fork right that is nominal, procedurally unusable, or conditioned on the consent of the party being left is likewise not an implemented analogue.

The corpus's interest in this is not comparative decoration. Fork is one of the mechanisms *Constitution, Not Cage* and *The Peership Thesis* rely on to distinguish a constitution from a cage, and it is a mechanism for which the corpus wanted a real-world precedent. It found none in the materials it reviewed. The point of recording that carefully is that the absence of a precedent is a **cost to the corpus's own position**, not a rhetorical asset.

## Result

**No implemented analogue of executable, unconfiscated fork was identified within the materials reviewed.**

### Results table (2026-07-13; re-confirmed 2026-07-14, no change)

| Instrument | Provides exit? | Executable in practice? | Unconfiscated (assets travel)? | Qualifies as a fork analogue? |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Ethiopia 1995, art. 39 | Yes — an express right of secession | Procedurally conditioned; no completed exercise under the clause is recorded in the reviewed material | Not established in the reviewed material | **No** |
| USSR 1977, art. 72 | Yes — an express right of secession | Read in the apparatus as an exit right that could be empty in practice; `kulikova2013ussr` is the scholarship relied on | Not established | **No** |
| St Kitts & Nevis 1983 | Yes — a secession provision for Nevis | Procedurally conditioned; no completed exercise is recorded in the reviewed material | Not established | **No** |
| UDHR 1948 | Yes — a right to leave a country | Declaratory instrument; not directly enforceable | Silent on what a departing party takes with it | **No** |

The general shape of the finding: these instruments establish that **exit rights can be written down and still be empty**, and that where they are not empty they are procedurally hedged and silent on what the departing party keeps. The sourcebook already records this as *mixed evidence*, and the corpus is explicit that it does not rest on these instruments' prestige. The secession clauses are cited as precedent for the **form** of a fork right, and as counterevidence about the **reliability** of one.

## The scope limitation is essential

**This is not a claim about all instruments that exist.** The reviewed set is five items. It was not produced by a systematic survey of the world's constitutions, corporate charters, treaty regimes, federal-exit provisions, cooperative or commons bylaws, or software-governance arrangements, any of which might contain something closer to an executable, unconfiscated fork.

The permitted form of the claim is the one the paper actually uses, narrowed at review:

> *"Among the constitutional secession clauses and emigration-rights instruments reviewed in the apparatus, no implemented analogue of executable, unconfiscated fork was identified; the claim is limited to those materials."*

The forbidden form is *"no implemented analogue of fork exists"* or *"no legal system provides for unconfiscated exit."* Nothing in this review supports either. **A single counterexample defeats the universal claim and leaves the scoped one standing** — which is the reason for the scope, and not a way of insulating the corpus from correction. A counterexample would be a genuinely valuable contribution to the program, because the corpus needs a working precedent more than it needs to be right about the absence of one. Report it: [issues](https://github.com/dan-lee-odinson/peership-corpus/issues).

## The query strings and selection criteria were not archived

**No reproducible protocol was archived for this review, and no queries were recorded.** The author closed this item **as-is** on deposit day (ledger **CL-T2** / **CL-T7**; sourcebook revision 1.3). No queries have been reconstructed after the fact; inventing search strings that were never run would be a fabrication.

For this file the gap is narrower than for the other two scans, and also different in kind. What **is** on the record is the reviewed set: the five instruments above are enumerated, individually cited, and independently checkable by anyone. What is **not** on the record is:

- **How that set was arrived at.** The selection criteria were not archived. Whether other candidate instruments were considered and rejected — and on what basis — is not recorded.
- **What was searched to find them.** No database, corpus of constitutions, or query is recorded.
- **What was searched and found nothing.** A scan's negatives are as informative as its positives, and they were not written down.

Consequences:

- **The review is verifiable but not reproducible.** A reader can check every claim made about each of the five instruments. A reader cannot check whether the five were the right five.
- **The finding is scoped to those five materials and dated to 2026-07-13.** It must never be widened by paraphrase.
- **The selection of the reviewed set is the weakest link in the chain**, and it is named here as such rather than left for a reviewer to find.
