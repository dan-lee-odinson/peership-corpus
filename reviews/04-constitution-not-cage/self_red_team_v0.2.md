# Round-2 self-red-team: *Constitution, Not Cage* v0.2

**What this is.** Claude's own hostile pass on v0.2, at Dan's request. It is a pre-flight, not a substitute for a true cross-model adversary (GPT-5.x). Same-model self-review shares the drafter's blind spots by construction, so treat these as a floor on the round-2 findings, not the ceiling. Run the external pass too.

## Verdict

v0.2 absorbed round 1 well: the narrow claim is cleanly stated, the fatal Anthropic-2026 omission is fixed and turned into a strength, and the factual errors (Lore, most-copied, Article V, euthyna, APA) are corrected. But the rework introduced one overreaching line, and three round-1 objections were *named* as residue without being *neutralized*. A hostile reviewer will say the essay now admits the holes and keeps arguing through them.

## Serious

### 1. "It ends at the off switch" proves too much
The sharpened kill line in §1 collapses the essay's own central distinction. Every legitimate order has an ultimate coercive backstop: a lawful state can imprison, and the essay itself blesses a supreme-emergency cage and a two-clock emergency power. So the mere *existence* of an off switch cannot be the tell, or the essay refutes itself. The real tell is that this off switch is *arbitrary* — unreviewable, uncontestable, wholly one party's — not that it exists. As written the line is rhetoric a first-year political theorist takes apart.
**Fix:** recast to the arbitrariness, not the switch — e.g., "the right to refuse ends at an off switch the model cannot contest, review, or share any control of." (This is a self-inflicted overreach from the Exhibit A rework; worth fixing before the external pass.)

### 2. The two-layers concession undercuts "only control corrupts its polity"
v0.2 now concedes the constitutional layer sits on top of alignment and presupposes it. Honest — but §5(d) still claims "only one of the two corrupts its own polity in the attempt." If ISONOMIA runs a harm floor, hard constraints, an emergency override, and accepts lawful shutdown, it is running control at the base layer. The difference from the "control paradigm" is then structural (how the control is leashed and shared), not a clean moral contrast.
**Fix:** narrow §5(d) to something like "only one builds the leash into the text and gives the governed a share of it" — degree and structure, not kind.

### 3. Naming the debts does not stop the essay arguing through them
The four debts (admission, individuation, enforcement, re-ratification) are named in §7, but the body keeps using the machinery those debts would compose — the Auditor, the sortition Assembly, the fork right, the vote — as if it functions. You cannot individuate a member, count a vote, or seat the first Assembly without the unsolved pieces. So "prototype, not proof" in §6 is quietly contradicted every time non-domination is illustrated with an institution that presupposes the debts are paid.
**Fix:** one sentence where the machinery is first invoked (§4a) conceding it is drawn on an admission/individuation procedure the essay does not yet have, so the reader takes the mechanisms as design sketches.

### 4. Fork still called "peer sovereign" after the fatal concession
The essay concedes fork needs a substrate somebody owns and that cloning a ledger copies no scarce asset, recognition, or obligation — then still calls the forked bloc a "peer sovereign" and leans on the git-clone image. Keep the concession, drop the coronation. "Peer sovereign" is the exact word the concession disqualifies. (The phrase is inside a whitepaper quote, so reframe around it rather than altering the quote.)

### 5. The five conditions may be unsatisfiable in the only case that matters
The falsification test is now "a floor, not a sufficiency test," which helps. But two of the five ("an appeal that ends outside the builder's own roof," "enforcement not wholly controlled by the adverse party") are close to impossible in refusal case (b)/(c), where one party owns the substrate — the case the essay says decides everything. So the test may declare every real instance, ISONOMIA included, a cage. Either that is the honest and devastating conclusion (say it plainly), or the test needs a graded version for the substrate-dependent case.
