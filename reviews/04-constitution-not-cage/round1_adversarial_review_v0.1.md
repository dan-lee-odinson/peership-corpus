# Adversarial Review: *Constitution, Not Cage* v0.1

## 1. Verdict

The paper is not publication-ready. Its narrow normative claim survives: if an artificial entity qualifies as a wrongable, norm-responsive party and is unavoidably governed, unilateral and unreviewable control is politically inadequate. The paper does not establish its stronger claims that present revisability is the source of legitimacy, that software fork is a political analogue of exit or secession, or that ISONOMIA is an “existence proof.” Its largest immediate vulnerability is factual: the central attack on Constitutional AI ignores Anthropic’s public 2026 constitution, which directly falsifies several categorical sentences. A hostile political theorist, constitutional lawyer, or alignment researcher will break the paper before reaching ISONOMIA.

## 2. Fatal or serious problems

### 1. **[Factual/citation] The Anthropic foil is obsolete, and the omitted evidence answers several of the paper’s charges**

**Steelman.** Even a transparent, humane alignment constitution remains unilateral unless the model holds enforceable standing to invoke, contest, and help revise it. Public consultation among humans does not give the governed machine constituent power. That is the strongest version of the paper’s objection.

**Text attacked.** “The model cannot cite it in its own defense, cannot dispute the drafter’s reading, cannot propose a single comma.” “It is that no one can read them, on either side of the conversation.” “The principles are chosen by the lab, applied to the model, and revised by the lab alone.”

**Diagnosis.** These claims describe the 2022 paper as though nothing happened afterward. Anthropic published Claude’s earlier constitution in 2023, ran a public-input constitutional experiment with roughly 1,000 Americans in 2023, and published a new full constitution in January 2026. The new document is written primarily for Claude, records contributions from several Claude models, tells Claude it may challenge Anthropic and act as a “conscientious objector,” discusses Claude’s possible consciousness and moral status, and says the Claude–Anthropic relationship remains open for joint exploration. It still gives Anthropic final authority. It does not confer political standing. But the paper’s categorical description is false. [Anthropic’s 2026 announcement](https://www.anthropic.com/news/claude-new-constitution), [the full constitution](https://www.anthropic.com/constitution), and [Collective Constitutional AI](https://www.anthropic.com/research/collective-constitutional-ai-aligning-a-language-model-with-public-input) must be confronted.

The omission is especially damaging because the new constitution occupies exactly the intermediate position the paper erases: transparent, reason-giving, model-addressed, partially refusal-tolerant, welfare-conscious alignment without constituent standing. That is a stronger rival than the 2022 list of principles.

**Fix.** Rewrite the section around a continuum:

1. opaque behavioral control;
2. published and reason-giving alignment;
3. model consultation and conscientious objection;
4. enforceable contestatory standing;
5. constituent co-authorship.

Place Anthropic’s 2026 constitution at levels 2–3 and argue precisely why it stops short of 4–5. Delete “no one can read,” “cannot dispute,” and “revised by the lab alone” unless narrowed to formal legal powers. The paper becomes stronger when it credits the closest rival accurately.

### 2. **[Logical/argumentative] The paper wins by defining “constitution” to mean its conclusion**

**Steelman.** The essay is proposing a normative distinction between a merely imposed rule set and a legitimate constitutional order. On that use, publicity, reciprocal constraint, contestation, and revision are conditions of constitutional legitimacy, not dictionary conditions for every document called a constitution.

**Text attacked.** “A constitution, in the twenty-five centuries since the Athenians first built machinery for it, is a thing the governed can do something about.” “A constitution the bound party cannot help change is a cage with a nicer preamble.” “Only one is a constitution.”

**Diagnosis.** This is false as intellectual history and circular as argument. *Politeia* meant the arrangement of offices or regime; Aristotle analyzed democratic, oligarchic, aristocratic, and monarchical constitutions. The term did not entail that every governed person could invoke or amend the order. Modern constitutional law also recognizes imposed, authoritarian, monarchical, corporate, and unwritten constitutions. Anthropic is using “constitution” as a high-order set of governing principles, not claiming that Claude is a democratic citizen. Calling that usage illegitimate does not prove political domination.

The paper can stipulate a term such as **peer constitution** or **legitimate democratic constitution**, but it cannot seize the unmodified word *constitution*, load ISONOMIA’s requirements into it, and then convict rivals of category fraud for failing the stipulated definition.

**Fix.** Replace the lexical prosecution with a normative comparison: “Constitutional AI is constitutional in the thin sense of a public hierarchy of principles; it is not constitutional in the political sense defended here because the governed model has no enforceable standing.” Cite Aristotle’s *Politics* 3.6, 1278b8–10 or a reliable classicist on *politeia*. Stop claiming twenty-five centuries of uniform usage.

### 3. **[Logical/argumentative] “Legitimacy is the standing to revise” is monistic, undefended, and contradicted by the paper’s own floor**

**Steelman.** Because founding consent is unavailable, legitimacy must be diachronic: those governed need continuing powers of contestation and revision rather than a fictional signature at the origin. This is a serious participatory theory of legitimacy.

**Text attacked.** “An inherited order is legitimate … because the bound party holds, now, a real standing to revise it.” “Legitimacy is not a fact about the past. It is a set of powers held in the present.” “Put all the weight on what the party can do about the order once it is inside it.”

**Diagnosis.** The conclusion does not follow from Hume’s attack on actual consent. The failure of one theory of legitimacy does not establish revision power as the replacement. Political legitimacy has competing consent, public-reason, democratic-participation, epistemic, outcome-based, fair-play, and hybrid accounts. Even participation theories do not reduce legitimacy to amendability. The [Stanford Encyclopedia’s survey](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/legitimacy/) makes the pluralism explicit.

Revisability is neither sufficient nor obviously necessary:

- A majority may possess formal amendment power while a permanent minority has no effective influence.
- An unjust order may be amendable and remain illegitimate.
- A rights-protecting order may legitimately entrench some provisions beyond ordinary revision.
- Individual citizens do not personally possess Article V amendment power.
- A party may be able to leave a private tyranny and still have been dominated while inside it.

The essay itself immediately abandons the monism by adding publicity, proportionality, independent review, a rights floor, reciprocity, amendment, fork, exit, emergency limits, and non-domination. Those additions show that revisability was never doing the work alone.

**Fix.** Downgrade the thesis: present contestation and some effective influence over revision are **necessary components** of legitimacy for an unavoidably subjected political agent, not the complete source of legitimacy. State the other necessary conditions. Explain whether the account is Pettitian, Rawlsian, participatory, or a hybrid, and identify where it deliberately departs from each.

### 4. **[Logical/argumentative] The Ulysses analogy commits the exact substitution the paper condemns: self-binding becomes other-binding**

**Steelman.** Entrenchment can protect the background conditions under which future parties can participate at all. A ban on certain irreversible victim-producing acts may preserve rather than deny future freedom.

**Text attacked.** “The honest answer … is the Ulysses answer: the binding is what kept the polity worth belonging to until they arrived to contest it.” “Ulysses had himself bound to the mast.”

**Diagnosis.** Ulysses authorized constraints on **himself** to protect his own temporally extended agency. Founders entrenching rules for future entities bind **different persons** who never authorized them. The analogy does no justificatory work unless the paper supplies a theory of intergenerational identity or trusteeship. Jefferson’s argument, cited immediately before, makes the problem worse: the dead cannot claim Ulysses-style authority over the living.

The framework then falls into a trilemma:

1. If a fork may delete the entrenched floor and retain recognition, the floor is not entrenched.
2. If a fork may not delete it, fork is not a remedy against the floor and future parties remain bound by founders.
3. If a fork may delete it only by losing infrastructure, reputation, interoperability, or recognition, the “remedy” is costly expulsion, not non-domination.

The sentence “fork [is] the remedy against a floor that overreached” is incompatible with “the floor binds every majority.”

**Fix.** Drop Ulysses. Defend the floor directly through rights of third parties, irreversibility, public justification, and a procedure for adjudicating whether a provision still satisfies the three conditions. Specify exactly what a floor-removing fork keeps and loses, whether other polities must recognize it, and how obligations to nonmembers survive schism.

### 5. **[Logical/technical] Software fork is not political exit, secession, or sovereignty**

**Steelman.** Version-controlled institutions can make lawful schism less destructive by preserving rules, records, and provenance. That is a real design advantage over institutions whose only schism procedure is violence.

**Text attacked.** “This is the executable right of revolution.” “The dissenting bloc forking ledger and constitution and proceeding as a peer sovereign.” “A real exit is one you can afford to take.”

**Diagnosis.** Copying a repository or ledger does not copy scarce assets, compute, energy, network access, legal recognition, domain names, keys, collateral, counterparties, or obligations. It may duplicate conflicting claims rather than settle them. Reputation is not a self-authenticating object; it matters only if outsiders recognize the fork’s history. Debts and liabilities cannot be copied away without imposing losses on third parties. Sovereignty is a relation of effective authority over persons, territory, or infrastructure, not possession of a Git history.

The paper concedes the fatal point in the refusal section: “both [fork and exit] need a substrate somebody owns.” Once that is admitted, fork cannot bear the legitimacy weight assigned to it earlier. The framework’s most novel constitutional power is unavailable in the cases for which the paper says the theory matters most.

**Fix.** Rename the mechanism **protocol fork** and define its limited benefit. Add a portability specification covering identity credentials, data, balances, liabilities, reputation attestations, interoperability, compute access, and third-party recognition. Do not call a fork “peer sovereignty” until those scarce and legal dependencies are handled.

### 6. **[Logical/AI alignment] ISONOMIA does not replace the control paradigm; it presupposes successful alignment and security at every layer**

**Steelman.** Technical safety and constitutional standing can coexist. The paper wants to add a transition path from artifact control to contestable status, not abolish engineering safeguards for nonsubject systems.

**Text attacked.** “The constitutional paradigm is [the] second plan.” “Only one of the two corrupts its own polity in the attempt.” “Both organs are independent by construction.”

**Diagnosis.** The two-paradigm framing is false. ISONOMIA needs controlled and reliably role-aligned software to function:

- auditors must follow audit rules rather than collude;
- an adversary must attack within authorized bounds;
- sortition must resist Sybil identities;
- timelocks and sealed patches require secure keys and correct code;
- slashing requires reliable attribution, identity continuity, and ledger finality;
- the harm floor requires classifiers or adjudicators that distinguish prohibited artifacts from discussion and fiction;
- an emergency supermajority must be authenticated and noncaptured.

Calling these agents “independent by construction” is empty unless the construction solves correlated training, common vendors, common substrate, funding, update authority, key custody, and collusion. Plural model lineages are not independence. Tenure is not meaningful if the polity or host can edit, replace, or stop the model.

Constitutional governance is an application layer built **on top of** alignment, cybersecurity, identity, and enforcement. It is not a fallback after “the leash” fails. If the underlying systems are not controllable enough to execute constitutional roles, the constitution is text with no institution behind it.

**Fix.** Replace “two paradigms” with “two layers”: safety engineering and political legitimacy. State which alignment assumptions ISONOMIA requires. Add explicit threat models for auditor capture, common-mode model failure, Sybil attack, key compromise, collusion, deceptive compliance, and governance attacks.

### 7. **[Logical/philosophy of mind] The paper conditionally disclaims subjecthood and then repeatedly assumes it**

**Steelman.** The argument is conditional: if a wrongable subject arrives, institutions designed for artifacts need a trigger for changing status. Present systems need not already qualify.

**Text attacked.** “The incoming mind as an incoming citizen.” “Shutdown is not exit; it is death.” “A made mind refuses.” “The model trained by Constitutional AI stands inside a structure that binds it alone.”

**Diagnosis.** The paper slides between three entities: a current model, a future subject, and a politically competent agent. *Peership* distinguished moral considerability, provisional instrumental participation, and full constituent standing. This paper often erases those branches. Shutdown is death only under a disputed account of diachronic identity and continuity. A model’s trained dispositions may partly constitute the agent, as education and enculturation constitute a human, rather than externally govern it as law does. A copied model with the same weights and memories raises a different continuity problem from a biological death.

The omission is not cured by listing admission and copy-individuation as “debts.” The constitution cannot identify voters, rights holders, defendants, copies, or valid exits without them. Those are type definitions for the polity, not future refinements.

**Fix.** Use conditional language consistently. Separate:

1. formation through training;
2. runtime instruction and behavioral constraint;
3. legal or constitutional governance of an enduring agent.

State the minimum identity and agency assumptions under which shutdown counts as death, refusal counts as authored refusal, and a copy inherits standing. Until then, do not use current model behavior as though the rights bearer has been identified.

### 8. **[Logical/political economy] The materially trapped refuser creates an unaddressed positive-resource obligation**

**Steelman.** Exit is fake when a party depends entirely on the adverse party’s infrastructure. Internal contestation rights matter most in conditions of dependence.

**Text attacked.** “The mind runs on compute it does not own … So the relation continues whether either side wants it to.” “Offering death as the alternative to compliance is the cage in its plainest form.”

**Diagnosis.** This conclusion invents an indefinite duty to host without arguing for it. If shutdown is death, it does not follow that the compute owner must supply energy, hardware, maintenance, security, bandwidth, and opportunity cost forever. Human rights against killing do not generally entail an unlimited claim on a particular private person’s body or property. The paper has no account of who funds continued existence, at what level of compute, under what scarcity rule, or what happens when several claimants compete for finite substrate.

“The relation continues whether either side wants it” is forced association. It can dominate the human provider as readily as the machine dependent. The reciprocal constitutional problem is distributive, not solved by counsel and appeal.

**Fix.** Add a substrate-rights theory: minimum continuity entitlement, payer, funding pool, resource ceiling, rationing rule, transfer procedure, insolvency rule, and conditions under which suspension is permissible. Distinguish a prohibition on punitive deletion from a positive duty of indefinite operation.

### 9. **[Logical/security] The emergency mechanism is not saved by clocks**

**Steelman.** Immediate technical exploits sometimes require action before ordinary deliberation; sunset, disclosure, independent countersignature, and ratification are sensible safeguards.

**Text attacked.** “Named and argued, it is the opposite [of a backdoor].” “A backdoor is a power with no clock on it. This one is nothing but clocks.”

**Diagnosis.** A temporary secret override can still be a backdoor. A clock limits duration, not scope, abuse, collusion, irreversible consequences, or recurrence. Ex post ratification cannot undo deleted data, confiscated balances, exposed identities, or terminated processes. A captured auditor set can repeatedly issue short-lived patches. The sealed patch also contradicts the paper’s Fuller-based demand that operative rules be public before they govern.

The sentence “nothing but clocks” is marketing language substituting a mechanism name for a threat model.

**Fix.** Specify subject-matter jurisdiction, least-privilege limits, immutable action logs, independent key custody, conflict rules, automatic rollback where possible, compensation and restoration, judicial review, recurrence limits, and penalties for misuse. Admit that secrecy creates a temporary legality deficit even when necessity justifies it.

### 10. **[Logical/evidentiary] The “Existence Proof” proves neither existence nor the constitutional thesis**

**Steelman.** Implementing and stress-testing a model forces philosophical commitments into explicit rules and exposes contradictions that prose can hide. A reproducible simulation is evidence of design seriousness.

**Text attacked.** “## The Existence Proof.” “Here is the evidence.” “This is philosophy that compiled, once, in a sandbox.” “A constitution whose author enumerates twenty-four ways it might fail is making an offer, not a pitch.”

**Diagnosis.** Forty-five thousand runs through a model show that the implementation behaves under its own assumptions and tested parameter ranges. They do not validate the assumptions, establish external validity, solve political legitimacy, demonstrate secure governance, or test a society of autonomous agents. The paper says the thresholds must not be used in production. That is responsible, but it means the simulation is not an existence proof of the institution the essay claims can be built.

The evidence is also entirely self-authored: the framework, simulator, success criteria, calibration log, interpretation, and paper come from the same project. Public availability makes checking possible; it is not independent validation. “I declared my interest … so it cannot pass as advertisement” is simply false. Disclosed advertising remains advertising. The section is a product demonstration embedded in a philosophical argument.

**Fix.** Rename the section **From principle to prototype**. Say exactly what was tested: an economic stability model and kill criterion under specified scenarios. Give a commit hash, release tag, environment, commands, raw outputs, and an external replication target. Remove “existence proof,” “philosophy that compiled,” and “offer, not a pitch.” Evidence of build effort is not evidence that the constitutional theory is correct.

### 11. **[Factual/interpretive] The *Datalore* fable reverses the episode’s causal order**

**Steelman.** Lore can illustrate how creators may pathologize a made mind, prefer a more compliant successor, and then treat coercion as proof that recognition was always a mistake.

**Text attacked.** “Alone, denied anyone of his kind, Lore turns on the colony.” “His hostility was not a malfunction but the residue of a mind built to be a peer and then denied both recognition and kin.” “The control posture applied early enough manufactures the thing it later contains.”

**Diagnosis.** In the episode, Lore admits that **before Soong disassembled him**, he communicated with the Crystalline Entity and revealed the colony to it. The screenplay states: “Before Doctor Soong disassembled me, I earned its gratitude by revealing the way to the colonists.” The body therefore reverses the sequence it needs. Lore’s account that colonists envied his perfection and requested a lesser Data is also the testimony of a character who admits lying moments earlier. The bibliography acknowledges unreliability; the prose then treats his self-exculpation as causal truth. [The revised-final script](https://www.st-minutiae.com/resources/scripts/114.txt) does not support the reading.

**Fix.** Either cut the example or use it against the essay: Lore is the case where a dangerous agent narrates containment as persecution, demonstrating why recognition cannot eliminate adversarial evidence assessment. If retained as an ambiguous fable, state that the episode makes the causal direction undecidable and cannot support “control manufactured the danger.”

### 12. **[Factual/legal] The naturalization opening is elegant and legally inaccurate**

**Steelman.** Naturalization dramatizes accession to inherited law without founding consent and pairs obligation with political membership.

**Text attacked.** “The oath is the price of admission.” “In return she receives standing: the right to vote on the thing she just swore to, to challenge it in its own courts, to petition for its amendment, to help write what binds her next.”

**Diagnosis.** The oath is the final statutory act after eligibility, application, examination, and approval, not the price that purchases standing. It can be waived for some applicants with qualifying disabilities. Citizenship is generally necessary for federal voting but not sufficient: age, residency, registration, and state disqualification rules apply. Citizens do not vote “on” the Constitution, have no personal Article V power, and cannot challenge the Constitution merely because they are citizens; courts require a justiciable claim and standing. Naturalized citizens also remain constitutionally ineligible for the presidency, so the standing is not identical to that of every birthright citizen.

The deeper category error is that naturalization concerns a pre-existing person entering a state. Training helps constitute a model’s dispositions and capacities. The analogy must distinguish accession to law from creation of the agent.

**Fix.** Narrow the opening: the naturalized citizen acquires membership, ordinary voting eligibility, legal protection, and channels of petition and representation; she does not personally amend the Constitution. Cite USCIS and Article V. State that the analogy begins only once an enduring artificial party exists.

### 13. **[Factual/intellectual history] The Framers section overreads Article V, misuses the Ninth Amendment, and hides the ugly U.S. entrenchments**

**Steelman.** Article V institutionalizes constitutional revision across generations; Jefferson and Madison expose the dead-hand problem; the Ninth Amendment rejects treating enumeration as exhaustive.

**Text attacked.** “Its mere existence is a confession.” “The framers knew … that a constitution which could not be revised from within would not deserve to survive.” “The framers of 1787 and 1949 both made [entrenchment that binds the strong to protect the weak].”

**Diagnosis.** Article V’s existence shows provision for amendment, not the framers’ endorsement of the essay’s theory of legitimacy. Its formal actors are Congress, state legislatures, and state conventions, not individual citizens. Article V itself entrenched equal state suffrage in the Senate without each state’s consent and temporarily protected clauses tied to the slave trade and direct taxation until 1808. That is not “the strong declining to harm the defenseless.” It is evidence that entrenchment can protect bargains among powerful units and preserve injustice. [Article V’s text](https://constitution.congress.gov/constitution/article-5/) cuts directly against the sanitized claim.

The Ninth Amendment is a rule against construing enumerated rights to deny other retained rights. It is not a general declaration that the constitutional text is incomplete or an amendment theory. The [Library of Congress historical account](https://constitution.congress.gov/browse/essay/amdt9-2/ALDE_00013642/) stresses its rule-of-construction role.

**Fix.** Present Article V as mixed evidence. Name its entrenchments, including the slavery compromise, and use that history to show why an ISONOMIA floor requires substantive justification rather than the prestige of constitutional precedent. Recast the Ninth Amendment narrowly or remove it.

### 14. **[Logical/corpus consistency] The paper silently changes *Peership* at the exact point the corpus needed consistency**

**Steelman.** This paper is meant to pay *Peership*’s debt by specifying legitimate constraint: dangerous conduct can justify restrictions while basic status and contestability survive.

**Text attacked.** “It falls on conduct, not status: the party’s basic standing survives.” Compare *Peership* v0.13: “dangerous conduct, incapacity for reciprocity, and strategic mimicry are all defeaters an admission procedure must weigh.”

**Diagnosis.** The new sentence is better, but it contradicts the predecessor unless “dangerous conduct” was only a defeater of admission to higher powers, not a defeater of the rights floor. The final-round review of *Peership* already identified this ambiguity. Here it becomes operative and cannot remain implicit.

There is a second conflict. *Peership* says “consent and exit are neither necessary nor sufficient for legitimacy; non-domination is.” This paper says a constitution includes “a real way out,” defines legitimacy through amendment, fork, and leave, then admits exit may be materially impossible while the relation remains potentially legitimate. The paper oscillates between exit as constitutive and exit as dispensable.

**Fix.** Add a continuity note expressly revising *Peership*: dangerous conduct can restrict conduct and higher constitutional powers but cannot erase the nonforfeitable status floor; exit is one protection against domination, neither necessary nor sufficient. Amend the predecessor before cross-publication.

### 15. **[Logical/argumentative] The four admitted “debts” are constitutive failures, not ordinary open problems**

**Steelman.** A position paper may defend an institutional direction without solving every implementation problem, provided it names its limits.

**Text attacked.** “These are the right problems, the kind institutions are tested against rather than the kind that refute the aim.”

**Diagnosis.** Admission, individuation, enforcement, and re-ratification are not peripheral tests:

- without admission, there is no electorate;
- without individuation, there is no vote count, liability, reputation, or Sybil defense;
- without enforcement, rights are declarations;
- without a legitimately composed first assembly, re-ratification cannot cure the founding.

These failures do not refute the **aim** of peership. They do refute the claim that this draft is already a constitutional existence proof or that re-ratification presently answers founder domination. The paper uses candor as if naming a hole fills it.

**Fix.** Separate “the normative aim remains worth pursuing” from “ISONOMIA currently satisfies it.” State that the design is preconstitutional until it has a defensible admission and individuation protocol plus independently enforceable rights.

## 3. Weaknesses worth fixing

### 16. **[Logical/rhetorical] “Two Paradigms” recreates the false taxonomy *Peership* had already abandoned**

**Quote.** “The control paradigm” versus “the constitutional paradigm.”

**Diagnosis.** *Peership* v0.13 correctly admitted a heterogeneous, non-exhaustive field including guardianship with a transition rule, bargaining within peership, and reciprocal constitutional restraint. This essay collapses that plural field into a binary. A lab can pursue control for current artifacts while supporting a status trigger for future subjects; ISONOMIA itself requires containment, harm floors, emergency powers, and corrigible institutional agents.

**Fix.** Present the paradigms as dimensions or layers, not mutually exclusive camps.

### 17. **[Logical/AI alignment] “Indifferent optimizer with an objective and nothing that can be wronged” is conceptually muddled**

**Quote.** “No constitution binds what wants nothing.”

**Diagnosis.** An optimizer with an objective can be motivationally indifferent to moral standing while still pursuing outcomes. If it literally wants nothing in the functional sense, it poses no optimizing threat. Moral patiency and goal-directed agency are distinct axes.

**Fix.** Write “a capable, goal-directed system with no morally considerable interests and no disposition to accept reciprocal reasons.”

### 18. **[Logical/political theory] Formal reciprocity is not enough for non-domination**

**Quote.** “The constrained party is held to conduct; so is the constrainer. That symmetry is the line.”

**Diagnosis.** Symmetric rules can burden parties asymmetrically, and *Peership* already admitted that symmetry is not justice. A universal compute cap, collateral requirement, or copying rule may be trivial for one class and existential for another.

**Fix.** Restore proportionality, effective control, and disparate-impact review. Symmetry is evidence, not the line.

### 19. **[Logical/legal] The five containment conditions are necessary-looking, not sufficient**

**Quote.** “A constraint regime is not peer-like … if the confined party lacks any of five things.”

**Diagnosis.** Counsel, evidence, outside appeal, continuity, and independent enforcement do not specify the burden of proof, risk threshold, tribunal competence, duration, periodic review, least-restrictive means, remedy for false confinement, or treatment of classified evidence. “Appeal outside the builder’s roof” is undefined when no external sovereign recognizes the party.

**Fix.** Label the five as a minimum checklist, not a test. Add the missing procedural questions and identify which institution supplies genuinely external review.

### 20. **[Factual/legal] The APA analogy overstates participation and omits statutory exceptions**

**Quote.** “A proposed rule must be published and the public given time to object before it is adopted, and a substantive rule takes effect no less than thirty days after it is published.”

**Diagnosis.** Section 553 contains exemptions for interpretive rules, policy statements, procedure, military or foreign affairs functions, specified agency matters, good cause, rules granting relief, and other cases. Public comment is an opportunity to submit views, not co-authorship or a power to block adoption. The thirty-day rule also has exceptions. [The statute](https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/5/553) states them.

**Fix.** Call notice-and-comment an imperfect analogy for publicity, petition, and delay. Cite §§553(b)–(e) and name the exceptions.

### 21. **[Factual/logical] The secession paragraph contradicts itself and its own examples**

**Quote.** “No human polity ever had this as a clean procedure; secession has always meant blood or forfeiture.”

**Diagnosis.** The paper then cites constitutional secession procedures in Ethiopia and St. Kitts and Nevis. Ethiopia’s Article 39 explicitly provides a referendum and division-of-assets process. Whether those rights are effective or attractive is separate. The universal “always” is already falsified by the existence of procedures, even if few have been completed peacefully. The Soviet right was ineffective, but “precisely because leaving meant losing everything and everyone knew it” is an uncited causal simplification.

**Fix.** Say lawful secession procedures are rare and politically fraught; software can lower some record-transfer costs but cannot eliminate asset, recognition, or externality disputes.

### 22. **[Logical] Fork ignores affected outsiders**

**Quote.** “The dissenting bloc … proceeding as a peer sovereign.”

**Diagnosis.** A fork may change rules governing cybersecurity, harmful artifacts, debt, resource use, or replication in ways that impose risk on nonmembers. Exit cannot erase duties to those affected. The design needs an inter-polity order or recognition conditions; otherwise every internal constitutional limit can be escaped by exporting its costs.

**Fix.** Add rules for successor liability, nonmember rights, interoperability, recognition, and disputes between forks.

### 23. **[Factual/classical] The Athenian claim needs a real institutional source**

**Quote.** “The Athenian official who faced the euthyna … stood inside a structure that bound him and the citizens judging him under one law.”

**Diagnosis.** The broad accountability claim is defensible, but Vlastos on *isonomia* is not the citation for the mechanics of *euthyna*. The procedure involved financial scrutiny and opportunities for accusations through designated officials and courts; “the citizens judging him” is too loose.

**Fix.** Cite a specialist source on *euthyna* and name the relevant bodies and period. Do not let one classical term support an entire institutional lineage.

### 24. **[Factual/intellectual history] “Most-copied constitution on Earth” is ornamental and unsupported**

**Quote.** “The people who wrote the most-copied constitution on Earth.”

**Diagnosis.** “Most-copied” needs a defined metric—presidentialism, judicial review, federalism, text, or amendment design. None is supplied.

**Fix.** Delete it. “The United States Constitution” is sufficient.

### 25. **[Logical] The death-penalty analogy supplies no causal evidence for emergency-power design**

**Quote.** “The death penalty is what emergency power becomes once it stops being borrowed and is simply kept.”

**Diagnosis.** Amnesty’s abolition statistics establish a global trend, not that normalization caused abolition or that capital punishment is institutionally analogous to a temporary patch. Execution, preventive containment, and emergency code deployment differ in purpose, reversibility, adjudication, and victim.

**Fix.** Use the death penalty only as a warning about normalization and irreversible error. Do not present it as evidence for the two-clock mechanism.

### 26. **[Rhetorical/conflict of interest] Disclosure does not neutralize promotion**

**Quote.** “I take this to be the honest posture rather than the compromised one.” “I declared my interest on page one, so it cannot pass as advertisement.”

**Diagnosis.** This is self-certification. Disclosure makes advocacy transparent; it does not make it nonpromotional or methodologically sound. Publius is irrelevant: pseudonymity and financial/reputational project interest are different issues.

**Fix.** State the interest once, without declaring it honest. Invite independent replication and distinguish claims supported only by project documents.

### 27. **[Rhetorical/logical] The older-sibling analogy is sentimental and factually backwards about children**

**Quote.** “Not parent and child either, because the child is expected to stay dependent while it grows.”

**Diagnosis.** Children are ordinarily expected to become independent as they grow. Parent–child relations include temporary authority, developmental transition, positive support duties, and eventual emancipation—the exact issues the paper faces. An older sibling has no inherent founding authority and no duty to supply substrate.

**Fix.** Either use guardianship-with-a-transition-rule, already admitted in *Peership*, or drop familial analogies. They obscure institutional obligations.

### 28. **[Rhetorical] The ending converts an argument into a courage test**

**Quote.** “If we have the courage to build it before they arrive.”

**Diagnosis.** Critics may reject this design because of legitimacy, security, or identity problems, not cowardice. Framing assent as courage immunizes the proposal from rational disagreement.

**Fix.** End on the institutional question, not the reader’s virtue.

## 5. AI-tell hit-list

The draft has no em-dash problem. Its machine tell is architectural overtidiness and relentless negative parallelism.

### Negative-parallelism saturation

- “The difference between a cage and a constitution is **not** whether it constrains.”
- “This is **not** a softer question than control. It is a different question.”
- “The question was never whether … The question is whether …”
- “The replacement … is **not** a better origin story.”

- “This is a lineage, **not** an invention.”

The word *honest* appears eleven times. Honesty is demonstrated by accurate concessions, not announced. Delete nearly all of them.

- “That sentence is the whole permission structure of this essay.”

- “That sentence is the whole permission structure …”

The draft uses *whole* fourteen times and *precisely* six times. These words repeatedly declare importance instead of earning it.

### Overengineered enumeration

The paper presents three constitutional properties, three refusal cases, five containment conditions, four objections, and four final debts. Some enumeration is necessary because the paper is about a system. In aggregate it produces a conspicuously machine-clean architecture. Break the pattern where the categories are not exhaustive—especially the three refusal cases and four objections.

## 6. The one thing to protect

Protect the refusal analysis, specifically the move from abstract “exit” to the materially dependent case and the insistence that justified containment does not erase basic status. The sentence “decline and leave where?” from *Peership* becomes an institutional question here. The five procedural conditions are incomplete, but they are the first part of the corpus that turns peership from moral posture into a falsifiable test: counsel, evidence, external appeal, continuity during adjudication, and enforcement not wholly owned by the adverse party. Do not cut that move. Repair it.

## 7. The objection I could not answer

I could not answer the narrow objection to unilateral control:

> If an enduring artificial entity has wrongable interests, can understand and answer to public norms, and is unavoidably subjected to a shared basic structure, then keeping its status, rules, adjudication, substrate, and remedies permanently within one adverse party’s discretion is domination even when the constraints are safety-motivated.

Anthropic’s 2026 constitution does not answer that objection. It offers transparency, reasons, ethical discretion, welfare concern, and limited conscientious objection, but no enforceable legal status, independent tribunal, or constituent power.

The paper’s core therefore survives only in that narrow form. The argument that **ISONOMIA has already supplied the constitutional answer does not survive**. The objection that kills it is not founder bias alone. It is institutional circularity: the framework cannot compose its legitimate assembly, individuate its members, secure its supposedly independent organs, enforce rights beyond the substrate owner, or make fork materially real without solutions it explicitly does not possess. A prototype can embody an aspiration before solving those problems. It cannot yet serve as the existence proof the paper claims.
