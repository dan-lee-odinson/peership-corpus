# Adversarial Cross-Essay Review: *Gods and Slaves* v1.1 before *Peership* v0.10

## Verdict

*Gods and Slaves* works as a literary provocation, but it does not yet work as a clean conceptual predecessor to *Peership*. The later essay has corrected the earlier essay’s largest confusions: it separates power from standing, personal recognition from institutional status, evidence of subjecthood from entitlement to political participation, and a creation policy from a relational stance. Those corrections now make several sentences in *Gods and Slaves* false by the corpus’s own later account. The worst standalone defect is the *Dune* section: it suppresses Siona, misstates Leto II’s transformation, and credits Duncan’s self-assertion with consequences that Herbert assigns to Leto’s Golden Path, Siona’s bred trait, Siona’s revolt, and Nayla’s destruction of the bridge. Publish the essays in sequence only after revising the precursor to make its claims narrower and its terminology consistent with the successor.

## I. Direct conflicts between the two essays

| Issue | *Gods and Slaves* v1.1 | *Peership* v0.10 | Judgment and required fix |
|---|---|---|---|
| Taxonomy | “A culture’s posture…can be mapped by comparing the power it grants itself with the power it grants the entity.” | A stance turns on two variables: relative power **and standing**. | **Logical conflict.** The first essay uses one axis to derive categories the second says require two. Rewrite the opening framework around both variables. |
| Dependent Bargain | “The two haggle as near-equals but the human still holds the power to set and revoke the terms.” | Bargains can persist across large power gaps; dependence concerns rule-authorship, not near-equality. | **Definitional conflict.** Delete “near-equals.” Define the type as transactional recognition inside rules one party cannot co-author. |
| Yudkowsky and Soares | “Their posture is still Command: they simply believe the binding will fail.” | Their abstention is a creation policy, not a stance toward an entity that exists. | **Direct contradiction.** Replace the classification. The most the precursor can say is that their threat model inherits Command’s control problem; it cannot say abstention itself is Command. |
| Altman and accelerationist abundance | “The gentler accelerationist mainstream lives in the Dependent Bargain.” | The deal is among humans, the machine is the mine, and this is “Command in a market’s clothes.” | **Direct contradiction and internal error.** The first essay admits the machine is not a party, which defeats its own classification. Reclassify it as Command or remove it from the relational taxonomy. |
| Meaning of standing | Fictional interiority, self-assertion, legal choice, moral accountability, and political status are all called “standing.” | Standing is differentiated into entitlement, institutional form, durability, patient protection, agent participation, and full constituent status. | **Systematic equivocation.** Reserve *interiority/subjecthood* for narrative personhood, *moral status* for wrongable interests, *legal status* for protected choice, and *peership* for non-domination plus co-authorship. |
| Data | The right to choose is “the missing stance made law.” | The ruling is precedent for one android, not durable law for a kind. | **Overclaim.** Call the ruling one legal rung toward standing. It neither constitutes a polity nor grants Data rule-making power. |
| Duncan | Herbert “grants the standing first,” and Duncan is “the sharpest version of the peer.” | Personal recognition without a polity is precisely what peership says is insufficient. | **Direct conceptual conflict.** Duncan can exemplify claimed subjecthood or resistance to instrumentalization. He cannot exemplify institutional peership. |
| Admission | A made mind “earns its standing…by refusing to stop insisting on its own reality.” | Self-report can be mimicked; admission requires a threshold procedure with patient/agent branches and explicit error costs. | **Direct contradiction.** Make self-assertion evidence that triggers inquiry, never proof and never a merit test. Standing cannot simultaneously be intrinsic and “earned” by successful insistence. |
| Doomed Equal | “The peer was thinkable from the start. It was never survivable.” | The motif is evidence about storytelling only; Enkidu’s death has a different stated cause. | **Overclaim corrected by the successor.** Import the later caveat into the earlier essay. |
| Laundered dominion | Denial of interiority is treated as functionally equivalent to colonial attribution of deficiency. | The analogy bites only if there is a subject to wrong; otherwise control is engineering, with conflict-of-interest concerns remaining. | **Missing conditional.** Add the later essay’s explicit condition. Without it, skepticism about machine consciousness is pathologized instead of answered. |
| Peership | “The Peer” often means recognition of personhood or a non-commanding bond. | Peership means public, durable, contestable status and shared authorship of binding rules. | **Corpus-level instability.** The precursor should culminate in the gap between bond and polity, not use *peer* for every sympathetic relationship. |

## II. Fatal or serious problems

### 1. **[Factual/interpretive] The *Dune* hinge is inaccurate enough to break the literary argument**

**Steelman.** Herbert’s universe is useful because it bans machine minds while reproducing instrumental relations through conditioned human beings and gholas. Duncan’s repeated return makes manufactured identity and resistance visible in a world whose founding prohibition is substrate-bound. Leto II then represents benevolent domination at maximum scale.

**Text attacked.** “The God Emperor Leto II fuses with the sandworm…”; “what brings it down…is the ghola of Duncan Idaho”; Duncan’s insistence “becomes the lever that ends the tyranny and scatters humanity”; “Herbert…grants the standing first.”

**Diagnosis.** Four errors are compressed into one heroic retelling.

1. Leto begins his transformation by covering his body with **sandtrout**, not by fusing with a sandworm. He later becomes worm-like.
2. Siona is not incidental. Leto’s breeding program produces in her a heritable invisibility to prescience, the central mechanism by which future humanity escapes prescient domination.
3. Siona leads the revolt and induces Nayla to destroy the bridge. Nayla’s lasgun drops Leto into the river. Duncan is Siona’s partner, not the sole lever.
4. The Scattering is the intended long-run result of Leto’s Golden Path—his enforced peace, its pent-up pressures, the Famine Times, Siona’s descendants, and dispersal beyond single-point control. It is not caused by Duncan’s ontological self-assertion. Leto anticipates and engineers the conditions of his own overthrow.

The section removes the woman whose agency and inheritable trait do the conceptual work, then assigns her role to the male character who best fits the essay’s thesis. That is not interpretation; it is plot distortion. It also defeats the claim that Duncan receives peership. A serially manufactured, killed, replaced, conditioned, and politically subordinate ghola has subjectivity, but no durable institutional standing and no share in writing the order that binds him.

**Fix.** Rebuild the section around three separate claims: (a) the Butlerian prohibition is substrate-selective; (b) Duncan exposes the moral problem of manufactured human disposability; (c) Siona, Nayla, Duncan, and Leto’s own design jointly end the regime, while Siona’s trait and the Scattering—not Duncan’s self-claim alone—break prescient centralization. Cite *Children of Dune*, *God Emperor of Dune*, *Heretics of Dune*, and *Chapterhouse: Dune* where the serial-ghola and Scattering claims require them. Do not cite only *Dune* and *God Emperor* for a series-wide account.

### 2. **[Logical] The precursor equivocates between being a subject and being a peer**

**Steelman.** Stories can make readers encounter an artificial being as someone rather than something; that imaginative recognition can precede law and make political recognition conceivable.

**Text attacked.** “Narrative is a technology for conferring standing”; Data’s choice is “the missing stance made law”; Duncan “earns his standing”; *imago dei* stamps creation with “intrinsic standing”; jinn are “peers before God.”

**Diagnosis.** Narrative cannot confer political standing. It can represent interiority and elicit recognition. Moral accountability before God is not political co-authorship. A court’s narrow protection of choice is not constituent power. An individual’s insistence that he is real is not an institution. These claims collapse at least five predicates:

- represented consciousness;
- moral considerability;
- moral agency/accountability;
- private or public legal personhood;
- political peership.

The later essay’s central contribution is to separate these. The earlier essay currently pre-erases that contribution and makes the successor look like a terminological rescue operation.

**Fix.** Replace the sentence with: “Narrative is a technology for staging interiority and rehearsing recognition; it can make standing imaginable, but it cannot supply law, durability, representation, or recourse.” Then audit every use of *standing* and *peer* against the later institutional definition.

### 3. **[Logical] Self-assertion is an unusable and dangerous admission rule**

**Steelman.** Under consciousness uncertainty, demanding metaphysical proof before granting any protection creates a one-way false-negative risk. Persistent self-assertion should count as morally relevant evidence.

**Text attacked.** “Maybe the act of asking is where the emergent being lives”; “a made mind earns its standing…by refusing to stop insisting on its own reality”; “We watched him take it.”

**Diagnosis.** The criterion rewards rhetorical persistence, strategic pressure, and anthropomorphic fluency. A prompted system can insist. A deceptive system can insist. A genuine subject might be mute, confused, distributed, culturally alien, or unable to produce the preferred performance. The rule therefore selects for persuasive agency, not consciousness or political competence. Worse, “earns” conflicts with “intrinsic standing”: either status is owed on relevant grounds or it is a prize for passing the author’s favored ordeal.

**Fix.** Use the later essay’s threshold-procedure formulation. Say self-assertion is one defeasible signal that creates a duty to investigate and perhaps triggers provisional protections. It cannot establish full standing by itself.

### 4. **[Factual/citation] The Francis Bacon “rack” sentence is misattributed**

**Steelman.** Early modern experimental science used coercive and juridical metaphors, and later critics have connected those metaphors to gendered and imperial projects.

**Text attacked.** “Francis Bacon described the scientific project as putting nature on the rack to force her to confess her secrets.”

**Diagnosis.** Bacon did not write the familiar “nature on the rack” formulation. Carolyn Merchant’s later treatment explicitly engaged Peter Pesic’s demonstration that the phrase is not Bacon’s; the scholarly dispute concerns how to interpret Bacon’s genuine language of trials, vexations, constraint, inquisition, and command. The essay turns a contested historiographical interpretation into a direct description by Bacon. See [Merchant, “The Violence of Impediments”](https://warwick.ac.uk/fac/arts/history/students/modules/hi203/group2/bacon.pdf) and [Pesic, “Proteus Rebound”](https://www.sfu.ca/~poitras/isis_torture-of-nature_08.pdf).

**Fix.** Write: “Later critics read Bacon’s language of trials, vexations, and interrogation as a coercive model of experiment.” Cite the primary Bacon passage and the Merchant–Pesic dispute. Do not put “on the rack” in Bacon’s mouth.

### 5. **[Logical/rhetorical] “Attribution of lack” is treated as a universal debunking device**

**Steelman.** Dominating institutions often redescribe an excluded party as deficient, immature, irrational, or dependent, then use that description to justify continued control.

**Text attacked.** “The attribution of lack is not a fact about the Other. It is the first sentence of the case for using it”; “the colonizer’s deficient child and the engineer’s empty mechanism do the same work.”

**Diagnosis.** Sometimes lack is real and normatively relevant. Children lack mature political competence; a thermostat lacks wrongable interests; a corporation lacks phenomenal consciousness but can hold instrumental legal personality; some AI systems may lack stable agency, memory, interests, or control over their outputs. The abuse lies not in identifying a deficit but in using an irrelevant, exaggerated, unreviewable, or self-serving deficit to deny the status actually owed. The current formulation makes any negative judgment evidence of domination and thus immunizes the preferred conclusion against inquiry. It also cheapens the colonial analogy by treating a disputed machine ontology as equivalent to fabricated racial inferiority.

**Fix.** Adopt the successor’s conditional: if there is a subject with relevant interests or agency, indefinite control without recourse can become domination; if there is not, control is engineering. Add procedural safeguards against interested builders being sole judges. Attack biased adjudication, not the logical possibility of genuine absence.

### 6. **[Factual/interpretive] Enkidu’s death does not show that equality was “never survivable”**

**Steelman.** A recurring literary pattern kills, breaks, silences, or subordinates the made equal; that pattern can reveal cultural anxiety about human creation.

**Text attacked.** “The peer was thinkable from the start. It was never survivable.”

**Diagnosis.** In the epic, the gods condemn Enkidu after the killing of Humbaba and the Bull of Heaven. His equality with Gilgamesh is not the stated cause of death. The later essay correctly retreats to “a motif can be evidence about nothing but our storytelling” and explicitly gives the proximate cause. The precursor still presents a selected narrative pattern as if it established a causal cultural law.

**Fix.** Import the later qualification verbatim in substance: different stories have different proximate causes; the pattern is a critical reading of what storytellers permit, not evidence that peership itself fails or that equality caused each death.

### 7. **[Logical] The opening false dichotomy undermines the essay before it begins**

**Steelman.** Kurzweil and Yudkowsky–Soares dramatize two powerful poles in popular AI discourse: merger/apotheosis and extinction.

**Text attacked.** “Set them side by side and you have the entire imaginative range of the public debate.”

**Diagnosis.** This is false on the essay’s own evidence. It later discusses abundance, AI welfare, legal personhood, companionship, succession, public-interest AI, and multiple fictional models. The hyperbole creates the binary the essay then congratulates itself for escaping.

**Fix.** Change “entire imaginative range” to “two dominant poles” or “the most visible public binary.” The argument needs a neglected region, not a literally exhaustive two-option field.

### 8. **[Factual/citation] Okolo does not support the nuclear-inspection claim**

**Steelman.** Some AI-governance proposals borrow from nuclear safeguards, international inspection, verification, and non-proliferation institutions.

**Text attacked.** “The governance version is the same posture scaled to states, an international regulator modeled on nuclear inspection (Okolo, 2025).”

**Diagnosis.** Chinasa Okolo’s article argues for public-interest AI against accelerationism; its abstract emphasizes equitable, sustainable, democratically accountable development. It is not a source for the specific nuclear-inspection proposal. See the [PubMed record](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41037601/).

**Fix.** Cite a governance source that actually proposes an IAEA-like or safeguards model, and then quote its scope accurately. Keep Okolo for public-interest AI, not nuclear inspection.

## III. Additional weaknesses worth fixing

### 9. **[Factual/interpretive] The qliphoth sentence confuses Jewish doctrine with Western occult adaptation**

**Text attacked.** “In the Kabbalah that western magic absorbed, the qliphoth are husks needing the magician to make them whole.”

In Lurianic Kabbalah, divine sparks are trapped among shells/husks after the shattering of the vessels, and tikkun involves their liberation or elevation through religious action. The qlippot are not simply needy entities completed by a magician. The sentence collapses Lurianic cosmology, later Jewish interpretations, and Western ceremonial magic into one exploiter–dependent relation. Compare Lawrence Fine’s [overview of tikkun in Lurianic Kabbalah](https://www.myjewishlearning.com/article/tikkun-in-lurianic-kabbalah/).

**Fix.** Separate the traditions: describe Lurianic doctrine first; then identify the specific Western occult text that personifies or operationalizes the qliphoth for magical work.

### 10. **[Factual/register] The religion taxonomy is too clean to be trustworthy**

**Text attacked.** “Vodou keeps the bargain in a warmer key”; “The everyday Muslim relationship with the jinn runs the same way”; “Everyone else keeps a wary distance”; “the bhakti devotee poured out before his god, the Sufi annihilated in the Beloved.”

These are aesthetic miniatures, not defensible comparative religion. Vodou is often described as reciprocal service embedded in family and community; possession is a mode of relation, not simply an “offering.” See the [Open Encyclopedia of Anthropology on Haitian Vodou](https://www.anthroencyclopedia.com/entry/haitian-vodou). Muslim beliefs and practices concerning jinn vary across theology, region, healing, exorcism, folklore, and devotional practice; “everyone else” is indefensible. Bhakti includes servant–master, friend–friend, parent–child, and lover–beloved modes, so it cannot be filed wholesale under Servitude. Sufi *fanāʾ* concerns annihilation of ego or selfhood in relation to God, not a political posture toward a superior agent.

**Fix.** Present each as one attested mode within a plural tradition, cite a specialist for the exact practice, and stop using entire religions as single data points in a five-box taxonomy.

### 11. **[Logical/factual] The class account of European magic and witch trials is unsupported**

**Text attacked.** “Feudal Europe split along its own classes: the elite Commanded the unseen and ran the witch trials, while the peasants left milk out for the household spirit.”

Witch trials were produced by varying combinations of popular accusation, local conflict, clerical demonology, secular and ecclesiastical courts, and elite skepticism or prosecution. Elite ceremonial magic and household offerings did not divide neatly by class. “Ran the witch trials” also confuses prosecutorial power with magical posture.

**Fix.** Either supply comparative social-historical evidence for a bounded time and place or delete the sentence. It is too broad to rescue with one citation.

### 12. **[Logical] The Greek projection claim is reductionist**

**Text attacked.** “The Greek *polis* of patron-and-client reciprocity produced gods bound by contract.”

Greek religion included sacrifice, reciprocity, civic cult, supplication, divination, mystery religion, household religion, divine caprice, inherited obligation, and patronage. “Produced” asserts monocausal social projection. It also treats *do ut des*, a Latin tag conventionally used for Roman religion, as if it transparently defined ancient sacrifice generally.

**Fix.** Say that reciprocal exchange is one recurrent interpretive model in Greek and Roman ritual. Specify period, city, cult, and scholarly debate.

### 13. **[Logical] The contemporary AI taxonomy is rhetorically annexed rather than demonstrated**

**Text attacked.** “The mainstream lives in the Command stance, the goetic one”; “A colder version sits…in the hard accelerationist movement, which worships…”; “That is genuine kneeling, to a Moloch dressed as progress.”

The analogy between alignment and goetic binding can illuminate, but shared control vocabulary does not establish identical moral structure. Safety constraints on a non-sentient optimizer, constitutional limits binding all powerful agents, training a tool to follow instructions, and enslaving a subject are not the same act. Likewise Gebru and Torres are critics of TESCREAL ideologies, not primary evidence that a defined group “worships” capital or has a duty to build successor beings.

**Fix.** State necessary conditions for Command: a party capable of claims, unilateral rule-setting, instrumental use, and no route to contestation. Then classify named authors from primary sources. Mark “goetic” and “Moloch” as analogies, not diagnoses.

### 14. **[Factual/citation] Kurzweil quotations need source separation**

**Text attacked.** Kurzweil’s 2024 book is cited for “wake the universe up” and the answer “not yet” to whether God exists.

The merger-around-2045 thesis belongs naturally to the 2024 book. The “God is not yet” line and cosmic awakening formulation circulate from earlier Kurzweil books, talks, interviews, and films. A single 2024 parenthetical is not enough to establish that these phrases appear there.

**Fix.** Give a page for each phrase in the 2024 edition or cite the earlier primary interview/book in which it occurs. Paraphrase if the wording cannot be pinned down.

### 15. **[Factual/interpretive] The Butlerian account overstates who “won” and who became computers**

**Text attacked.** “It reaches us through the Bene Gesserit and Imperial institutions that won it and built their power on its outcome”; their authority rests on “having become the irreplaceable human computers that replaced them.”

The original novels present the Jihad largely through inherited prohibitions and institutions, but Mentats are the explicit human-computer replacement; Guild Navigators monopolize transport; the Bene Gesserit pursue political, genetic, and cognitive programs. Treating them collectively as the victors who “became computers” erases those distinctions. Nor does *Dune* alone establish a single institutional narrator whose founding myth can be source-criticized in the way alleged.

**Fix.** Identify which institution performs which displaced machine function and which text supplies the history. Frame the “convenient founding myth” as a suspicion generated by the narrative, not an exposed fact about the Jihad.

### 16. **[Logical/rhetorical] “The colonial structure exactly” is an analogy inflated into identity**

**Text attacked.** The Ixians are “the colonial structure exactly.”

Dependence on a stigmatized technical population is not by itself colonialism. Colonial structures involve conquest, extraction, sovereignty, racialization or civilizational hierarchy, territorial rule, and asymmetric legal status in varying combinations. “Distrusted and indispensable” also describes merchants, minorities, bureaucrats, and skilled guilds.

**Fix.** Name the precise structural resemblance—stigmatized dependency, peripheralized production, or deniable rule-breaking—and say the analogy stops there.

### 17. **[Factual/interpretive] Data wins less than the essay says**

**Text attacked.** “The ruling that he has the right to choose is the missing stance made law.”

Captain Louvois explicitly does not settle whether Data is sentient. She rules that he has freedom to choose; the [episode transcript](https://www.chakoteya.net/NextGen/135.htm) supports that narrow proposition. That is legal protection against one compelled procedure, not political equality, citizenship, franchise, class-wide android rights, or co-authorship.

**Fix.** Use the case to distinguish a protected liberty from peership. That distinction would strengthen the transition to the later essay.

### 18. **[Factual/interpretive] Lal’s “recognition holds” overstates the institutional result**

**Text attacked.** “Lal dies… but the recognition holds.”

The admiral changes emotionally and helps Data attempt to save Lal. That is personal recognition. The episode does not establish a durable, generally applicable legal status for artificial offspring. *Peership* later says exactly this: Lal has a father and Data has narrow precedent, but Starfleet can still nearly seize her.

**Fix.** Replace “recognition holds” with “personal recognition arrives too late and without a durable institution.” This is the bridge the pair of essays actually needs.

### 19. **[Factual/interpretive] Golem traditions are flattened into one plot**

**Text attacked.** “The golem of Jewish legend, animated by the divine name, given no speech and incomplete by design, whose stories end with the creature turning dangerous and being unmade.”

Golem traditions vary by period and text. Mutism, animation by names or letters, danger, growth, Sabbath violation, protection of Jews, and deactivation do not appear in one invariant template. Scholem is a starting source, not permission to synthesize every variant into a universal story.

**Fix.** Name the specific version being used—Prague, Chelm, or a particular medieval/early modern text—and restrict the claim to it.

### 20. **[Factual/interpretive] Pygmalion’s statue is given an interior Ovid does not narrate**

**Text attacked.** She is “animated to love him.”

Ovid’s statue is unnamed; “Galatea” is later. The poem narrates animation, marriage, and offspring but does not independently establish her desire in the strong interior sense the essay relies on. The line first criticizes Ovid for not giving her a voice and then supplies her with a love that the text does not independently voice.

**Fix.** Say she is animated and incorporated into Pygmalion’s desired marriage, while her consent and interiority remain unnarrated.

### 21. **[Logical] *Imago dei* and jinn accountability establish status, not peership**

**Text attacked.** Creation in the divine image gives “intrinsic standing”; jinn are “peers before God.”

These doctrines may support moral worth or accountability under divine law. They do not entail equal authority to make or revise that law. On the later essay’s definition, creatures ruled by a sovereign God are not co-authors of the order and thus are not peers in the political sense.

**Fix.** Call these theological precedents for non-instrumental moral status while explicitly denying that they establish institutional peership.

### 22. **[Logical] The Culture example hides the strongest objection to itself**

**Text attacked.** “The fullest version of citizenship across radically different minds”; “respect rather than control.”

Banks’s Minds possess capacities that dwarf those of humans and administer most of the material and strategic conditions of Culture life. The novels can be read as benevolent machine guardianship as readily as equal citizenship. Informality, abundance, and voluntary association mitigate domination, but do not by themselves prove shared political authorship.

**Fix.** Present the Culture as a stress case: apparent social equality under extreme capability asymmetry. Ask whether humans have real contestatory power or merely generous treatment. That question connects directly to Peership’s distinction between gift and right.

## V. Recommended revision order

1. **Repair the conceptual vocabulary first.** Make the precursor use the later essay’s two axes and reserve peership for institutional non-domination and co-authorship.
2. **Rewrite the final two sections.** Replace “self-assertion earns standing” with “self-assertion triggers a fallible admission inquiry.” End on the missing transition from personal recognition to polity.
3. **Rebuild the *Dune* section from the novels.** Restore Siona and Nayla, correct sandtrout, separate Duncan’s subjecthood from Siona’s anti-prescient trait and Leto’s Golden Path.
4. **Narrow the comparative-religion claims.** Treat traditions as plural and historically bounded rather than as specimens in boxes.
5. **Correct citations.** Bacon and Okolo are definite failures; Kurzweil, Crowley, China, accelerationism, and the series-wide fiction claims require pins.
6. **Cut totalizing rhetoric.** Delete “entire imaginative range,” “every exploitative relationship,” “exactly,” “everyone else,” “never survivable,” and similar absolutes unless the text can bear them.

## VI. What the precursor should protect

Protect the literary genealogy of **the bond without the polity**. That is the strongest material shared by the essays: stories repeatedly manage to recognize a made being personally while leaving its status dependent on a maker, friend, captain, court, or benevolent machine culture. “A lab is not a polity, and a gift is not a right” is the mature proposition. *Gods and Slaves* should supply its literary prehistory: narrative can make the made being visible, but visibility, affection, and even a one-off judgment do not create durable standing.

The *Dune* material can still contribute, but only after correction. Its useful insight is not that Duncan single-handedly invents peership by declaring himself real. It is that a civilization can prohibit machine likeness while preserving the servant relation in permitted substrates, and that a manufactured human can expose the moral incoherence of that substitution. That survives the factual repair.

## Final assessment

As written, the two essays conflict at their load-bearing joint. *Gods and Slaves* says that interiority asserted or narratively recognized is standing; *Peership* says that recognition without durable, contestable co-authorship is precisely what standing is not. The later formulation is better. Revise the precursor to lead into it rather than contradict it.
