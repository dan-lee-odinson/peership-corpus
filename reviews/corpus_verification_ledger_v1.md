   **Mining pass (Dan asked what else is usable — five finds, in descending value):**
   1. **The unequal-laws point, p. 350** — "equality before the law" fails as the full meaning because "unequal laws, i.e. laws which sanction the unequal distribution of political rights and privileges to different social classes, might be upheld quite 'equally,' i.e. impartially." Strongly recommend using it.
   2. **Herodotus III.83.1 anchor, p. 337 n. 5** — Vlastos reads isonomia there as "the writer's name for the form of government espoused by Otanes." Direct scholarly support for CnC's revised 3.83 coda (Flag 8) and for Peership's "the older word Otanes reached for."
   3. **Alcmaeon fragment 4, pp. 362–363** — the oldest extended use of the word outside politics: health is the *krasis* of equal powers; "the 'monarchic' preponderance of any power is a diseased condition, destructive of the organism," patterned on the democratic concept, with Anaximander's "equalitarian order... normative for the universe" behind it.    4. **The isomoiria contrast, p. 352** — Greek had a separate word for "equal shares" (*isomoiria*) and the democrats did not choose it; what isonomia equalizes is standing, not distribution.
