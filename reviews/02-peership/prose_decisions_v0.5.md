# Prose decisions for you — Peership v0.5

You asked me to keep the rhetorical prose intact unless the reviewer flagged that specific line, and to let you decide on the flagged ones. In v0.5 I already changed the flagged lines whose problem was a *claim* (e.g., "not slaughter but assimilation" — the negative-parallelism was carrying an overstated fact, so hedging the fact rewrote the line). What remains below are the lines the reviewer flagged as *pure style/voice* — where the prose itself is the only thing at issue. I left every one of these AS WRITTEN in v0.5. Your call on each.

For each: the line, the reviewer's objection, and my honest read.

---

### 1. The four-word forecast quartet
**Line:** "Extinction, succession, apotheosis, abundance."
**Reviewer:** compresses incompatible positions into a "marketing-ready quartet."
**My read:** Keep it. It's doing real structural work (it sets up the "what will it do to us / what will we be to each other" turn), and the compression is the point, not a cheat. This is your voice at its best. Reviewer is applying journal norms to an essay.

### 2. "about the body count"
**Line:** "...about the road ... about the date, about the body count."
**Reviewer:** "cheap heat"; the real disputes are broader (definitions, feasibility, governance, consciousness).
**My read:** Half-right. In v0.5 I already broadened the surrounding sentence ("what 'AGI' even names, how far off it is, and what it would cost us"), so "body count" is now softened to "what it would cost us." If you want the punch back, restore "the body count" as the last item — it's defensible now that the sentence around it is fuller. Your taste.

### 3. The reader-commands
**Lines:** "Read it carefully," "Hold those two facts together," "So say the hard thing plainly," "Step back from the occupants to the map," etc.
**Reviewer:** repeated stage-directions "simulate decisiveness and become mannered"; also reads as an AI tell.
**My read:** This is genuinely your reflective register (the POA voice directs the reader), but the *density* was too high. In v0.5 I thinned them by roughly half and varied the ones that remain. I think it's now in the right zone. If you read it aloud and it still feels like being poked, cut two more. Flagging so you can judge by ear.

### 4. "Scott's peasants, in weights."
**Line:** removed in v0.5.
**Reviewer:** "stylish but intellectually fraudulent" — a sociological analogy posed as an ML mechanism.
**My read:** I cut it, because its problem was inseparable from the overstated "deceptive alignment is established" claim, which I hedged. If you want the compression back, it can return ONLY as an explicit simile after the hedge — e.g., "...the oldest sociology there is; the peasants' play, now in weights." Offered, not inserted. Your call whether the punch is worth re-approaching the line the reviewer hit hardest on style.

### 5. "They were welcomed in and metabolized. Two percent."
**Line:** removed/reworked in v0.5.
**Reviewer:** "strong copy, bad science" — assigns intentional welcome and a single causal process.
**My read:** This was a casualty of the causal→analogy reframe of the whole Neanderthal section, which you approved in principle. The current version makes the same emotional point ("dissolved across billions of genomes, no living people, no whole lineage") without the causal overclaim. If you miss the two-word gut-punch ending, "Two percent." could return as a standalone line after the hedged sentence — it's just a number, so it survives the science objection on its own. Tell me and I'll place it.

### 6. "The fossil record and the folklore rhyme."
**Line:** kept, lightly, in the Doomed Equal section ("our species did in fact end up the only living claimant to it").
**Reviewer:** a "significance-inflating bridge between incomparable evidence classes."
**My read:** I demoted it from a standalone aphorism to a subordinate clause, which removes the "these two very different things prove each other" implication the reviewer objected to. If you want the crisp aphorism back as its own sentence, it's your voice and I'd support it — but then add half a sentence making clear you mean they *echo*, not *corroborate*.

### 7. The closing line
**Old line:** "The fifth has been waiting four thousand years for a maker brave enough to let the made thing live."
**Reviewer:** "sounds like a manifesto launch line, not a conclusion licensed by the argument."
**My read:** This is the biggest voice call in the essay, and I did NOT keep it in v0.5 — the new close ends on "Whether we can build the fifth is the question the rest of this work takes up," which is honest and sets up the sequel. BUT: the old line is genuinely great, and the reviewer's objection ("not licensed by the argument") was really an objection to the *exclusivity overclaim*, which v0.5 has now fixed. So the line may be earned again. Three options:
  - **(a)** Keep the sober v0.5 ending (my default for Direction A — it reads as philosophy, not launch).
  - **(b)** Restore the original manifesto line as the very last sentence, after the sober one. High risk/high reward: it's the most quotable thing you've written and the most "product-launch" thing you've written, which cuts both ways given you're preparing the ground to name ISONOMIA.
  - **(c)** A middle line that keeps the cadence without the manifesto ring — e.g., "The fifth stance has waited a long time for a maker willing to let the made thing stand." Softer "stand" (echoes "standing") instead of "live," less battle-cry.

I recommend (a) for this philosophical piece and saving the manifesto line for the actual launch essay, where a battle-cry is register-appropriate. But it's exactly the kind of call that's yours, not mine.
