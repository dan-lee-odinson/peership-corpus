# Confirmation Audit: *Peership* v0.10

**Draft reviewed:** `peership_draft_v0.10.md`  
**Comparison:** v0.9 objections against v0.10 revisions  
**Result:** **SUBSTANTIVE PASS; PUBLICATION-READINESS FAIL**

## 1. Verdict

The central argument now holds in its reduced form. V0.10 resolves the standing/peership contradiction, the Bargain taxonomy failure, the capacity-test problem, the refusal/exit evasion, the impossible individual-veto definition, and the overclaim that reversal uniquely proves one institution. Peership is now coherently defined as a family of reciprocal, non-dominating arrangements whose justification does not change merely because the power ratio reverses; that is a defensible claim. The remaining logical defects are local: the conclusion briefly restores the malformed “stronger party holding the stance” formula, and “representation short of full franchise” is admitted into peership without saying when representation is constituent rather than guardianship. The draft is not ready to deposit because it retains eleven explicit verification flags and still lacks sources for Alcidamas, the Stoics, LeCun 2022, several quotations, and several field-wide claims.

### 1. **(b) Logical/argumentative — The conclusion reintroduces the reversal error the body just fixed**

> “Servitude and Appeasement are the stances of the weaker party already, and inverted they ask a stronger party to surrender or to placate…”

This is the correct repair in paragraph 57.

> “The postures the debate crowds into are all keyed to who ends up stronger, and none is one we could bear a stronger party holding toward us…”

This sentence in paragraph 107 restores the old malformed test. A stronger party does not characteristically hold Appeasement or Servitude toward a weaker one; those postures presuppose the holder’s weakness. The conclusion therefore contradicts the main argument’s careful reformulation.

**Fix:** Replace the clause with:

> “The postures the debate crowds into all depend on occupying a particular side of the power relation, and none supplies a reciprocal relation whose justification survives role reversal.”

That preserves the actual result without asking a stronger party to adopt a weaker-party stance.

### 2. **(b) Logical/argumentative — “Representation short of full franchise” is not automatically peership**

> “What survives the screen is a *family* of reciprocal, non-dominating arrangements: capability-proportionate constitutionalism…, representation short of full franchise, enforceable bargains between parties that jointly author their terms.”

**Steelman:** Peership is scalar. Meaningful representation, agenda access, judicial contestation, or group voice can supply co-authorship without one-agent-one-vote.

**Strike:** Representation can also be the defining institution of protected subordination. Children, wards, noncitizen residents, and persons under guardianship may be represented without being peers. The essay’s central distinction is precisely that protection and representation do not necessarily yield co-authorship. By inserting undifferentiated “representation short of full franchise” into the peership family, paragraph 57 blurs the line paragraph 35 worked to establish.

**Fix:** Qualify the member of the family:

> “representation with independent agenda-setting, contestation, and revision powers, even where it falls short of an individual franchise”

Alternatively, call representation a transitional component rather than peership itself. The criterion must be whether the represented constituency can hold the representative and rule-makers accountable, not whether someone speaks on its behalf.

### 3. **(b) Logical/argumentative — The draft still conflates moral entitlement with legal right**

> “A status revocable at the grantor’s pleasure is not thereby a non-right; it is an ineffective right, a claim owed but unenforced.”

**Steelman:** A moral right remains owed even when institutions fail to recognize or enforce it. Revocability does not erase the underlying entitlement.

**Strike:** A discretionary company benefit may be neither an ineffective legal right nor a legally recognized status. It can be a privilege with no correlative duty. The sentence is true only if “right” means moral claim, while the surrounding paragraph moves among entitlement, status, recognition, and enforcement. The distinction was added to eliminate circularity; this residual equivocation risks recreating it.

**Fix:** Write:

> “A moral claim may remain owed when law neither recognizes nor enforces it. A discretionary benefit, however, is not yet a legal right against its grantor.”

Then reserve **entitlement** for the moral claim, **legal status** for public recognition, and **durability** for resilience in practice.

### 4. **(a) Factual/citation — The draft cannot be published with its current verification debt**

The references contain eleven explicit `[verify]` markers and additional unresolved notes without the tag. That is an honest drafting practice, not a publishable bibliography. More importantly, the body still makes sourced-looking claims for which no adequate entry exists:

**Fix:** Clear the verification list in section 4 below. Until then the confirmation result is **argument pass, deposit fail**.

## 3. Weaknesses worth fixing

### 1. **(b) Logical/argumentative — “Worth investing in” is still one inference beyond the argument**

> “Peership is the one part of the map worth investing in under deep uncertainty…”

The preceding sentence correctly denies that symmetry proves safety, feasibility, or best expected value. “The one part worth investing in” then sounds exclusive again. Prevention, technical control, monitoring, bargaining institutions, and welfare research may all be worth investment even if they are not final relational ideals.

**Fix:** Say “one neglected part of the map worth investing in” or “the only final-status ideal in this repertoire whose justification does not reverse with the ratio.”

### 2. **(c) Rhetorical/register — “The debate has abandoned it” is unsupported drama**

> “The debate has abandoned it. That is the mistake.”

The essay itself cites AI-rights, suffrage, welfare, relationality, and constitutional proposals. The field may neglect peership as defined here; it has not abandoned every adjacent idea.

**Fix:** “The debate has not developed it as a serious institutional program.” That is narrower and consistent with the cited counterexamples.

### 3. **(a) Factual/citation — The opening remains overclaimed**

> “The race … has crossed … into a timeframe its own participants now measure in years.”

Grace et al.’s aggregate 2047 forecast is roughly two decades away and surveys researchers, not necessarily “participants” running an AGI race. The AAAI result accurately reports skepticism that scaling current approaches alone will yield AGI. Neither supports the field-wide claim that “the live question has turned from whether.”

**Fix:** Attribute the short forecasts to named lab leaders or remove the first claim. Replace the trend claim with “Questions of when, how, and on whose terms now occupy a substantial part of the literature.”

### 4. **(b) Logical/argumentative — The Command mechanism still outruns the evidence**

> “The overseer and censor and monitor scaling with the capability of the controlled…”

Morgan illustrates the joint development of freedom and slavery in colonial Virginia; he does not establish a general scaling law between monitoring cost and capability. Kuran supplies a genuine source for preference falsification, but “coerced parties stop sending honest signals” remains too categorical.

**Fix:** Present both as mechanisms or hypotheses: “control often creates continuing monitoring costs” and “coercion can induce preference falsification.” Add comparative empirical work if the universal wording matters.

### 5. **(a) Factual/intellectual history — The classical section still needs scholarly anchors**

The conceptual cautions are now good: no direct Aristotle–Alcidamas rebuttal is claimed, *isonomia* chronology is contested, and no continuous bloodline is asserted. But “Stoics widened the impulse into a cosmopolis of all rational beings” does not imply political equality or franchise, and “Athens made equal standing operational” remains an interpretation requiring modern scholarship.

**Fix:** Add a fragment source for Alcidamas, modern work on *isonomia*, and a Stoic source. Call Stoic cosmopolis a moral-cosmopolitan extension unless political equality is separately established.

### 6. **(a) Factual/citation — Armitage is not enough for every Locke claim in the sentence**

> “Locke’s consent theory coexisted with investment in the slave-trading Royal African Company and a hand in the Fundamental Constitutions of Carolina…”

Armitage’s article is real and correctly cited as *Political Theory* 32(5):602–627, DOI 10.1177/0090591704267122. It is a strong source for Carolina and the relationship to the *Two Treatises*. The compound sentence also needs a precise source for Locke’s Royal African Company investment and exact pages for the disputed authorship claim. See [Armitage’s publication record](https://armitage.scholars.harvard.edu/publications/john-locke-carolina-and-two-treatises-government).

**Fix:** Split the claims and pin-cite each. Consider adding the later dedicated scholarship on Locke, slavery, and authorship rather than making one article carry the whole sentence.

### 7. **(b) Logical/argumentative — The Neanderthal section is now accurate but dispensable**

The section no longer claims that lack of institutions caused absorption, no longer calls Neanderthals a political people, and no longer presents one lineage as a comparative record. Its only inference is that merger requires a criterion of identity continuity. That inference is valid but does not require the archaeological catalogue.

**Fix:** Keep it if the corpus needs the paleoanthropological motif; cut it if argumentative efficiency matters. It now illustrates rather than supports the thesis.

### 8. **(c) Rhetorical/register — The companion-work funnel remains visible**

> “The companion work argues how.”

The new falsifiable requirements in paragraph 63 substantially repair the bait-and-switch. The promotional sentence is therefore unnecessary.

**Fix:** End the institutional discussion with the four requirements. Let the bibliography or an author note identify the companion work.

## 5. AI-tell hit-list

V0.10 materially improves the register: it cuts roughly 750 words from v0.9, retains zero em dashes, sharply reduces “I will” scaffolding, removes the AI-coauthor aside, and deletes most explicit discussion of earlier drafts and reviewer objections. The remaining tells are concentrated and removable.

- > “Two problems follow, and both are genuinely hard rather than rhetorical.”

  Self-certification. Delete “and both are genuinely hard rather than rhetorical.”

- > “So the honest shape of the claim…”

  “Honest” is audit voice. Use “The resulting claim is…”

- > “That is the honest version of the essay’s claim…”

  Same tell two paragraphs later.

- > “It is not thereby proven safe, feasible, or best in expected value…”

  Necessary limitation, but the immaculate rule of three reads model-polished. A plainer sentence would sound less defensive.

- > “The claim is not that these are solved, or all solvable. It is that they are the right problems…”

  Repeated “not X; it is Y” correction pattern.

- > “The problems are open, some may not yield, and the work is worth doing.”

  Tidy concession–concession–uplift cadence.

- > “One modest thing follows and no more.”

  Reviewer-facing scope control. State the merger warning directly.

- > “The bond was never the missing piece. The polity was.”

  Effective once, but unmistakably slogan-shaped.

- > “A lab is not a polity, and a gift is not a right.”

  This is the strongest slogan in the essay. Keep it and cut competing slogan endings.

**Global edit:** remove the remaining “honest,” “not X but Y,” and self-certifying limitation phrases where the substantive sentence already makes the limitation clear. The prose is now much closer to the stated human voice; do not re-expand it.

## 6. The one thing to protect

Protect the three-part distinction and its compressed statement:

> “Entitlement… Institutional form… Durability…”

> “Power secures peership. It does not constitute or justify it.”

That is the conceptual center that survived the full adversarial sequence. V0.10 successfully builds the admission branch, refusal analysis, and non-domination definition around it. Cutting it would reopen the original standing/peer contradiction.

## 7. The objection I could not answer

The core survives. I could not defeat the claim that a reciprocal, non-dominating political relation has a justification that does not change merely because humans and artificial agents exchange positions in the power hierarchy, while Command, Dependent Bargain, Appeasement, and Servitude each presuppose a particular position in that hierarchy. Nor could I defeat the narrower practical conclusion that this makes peership a neglected research program worth developing before any qualifying artificial constituency exists.

The strongest surviving counter does not kill that core: peership may prove infeasible, admission may remain epistemically indeterminate, and coercion may erase the distinction between constitution and cage. V0.10 now states those as failure conditions and research requirements instead of pretending they are solved. That is enough for the essay’s bounded conclusion. It is not enough for an institutional proposal, but this essay no longer claims to be one.
