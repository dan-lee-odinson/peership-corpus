## Edit 1 — natural-slavery wording (§ The Oldest Argument, first paragraph)

**BEFORE:**
> Aristotle's *Politics* opens with natural slavery: some people are "living tools," animate instruments, slaves by nature because they can perceive reason without holding it (Aristotle, *Politics* 1.1254a).

**AFTER:**

## Edit 2 — the shuttle quotation (same paragraph)

**BEFORE:**
> And he attached an astonishing aside: if "the shuttle would weave and the plectrum touch the lyre without a hand to guide them," masters would not need servants and craftsmen would not need assistants (*Politics* 1.1253b–1254a).

(Note the clause order: Reeve has craftsman/assistants first, masters/slaves second — v0.11 had it reversed. The Daedalus/Hephaestus clause is optional but it is in the same sentence in Reeve and it is good company; cut if it crowds the line.)
