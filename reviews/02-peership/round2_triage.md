**A2. Finding 1 — the missing bridge from agency to standing. ACCEPT, with the reviewer's own premise.** This is the review's best objection and its fix is a gift: the all-subjected principle. Drafted addition (one paragraph, § The Fifth Stance, after the entitlement/form/durability passage):
   > What creates the claim is not capability but subjection. An entity that (a) has interests capable of being wronged, (b) can understand and answer to public norms, and (c) is enduringly and unavoidably subject to a shared basic structure has a defeasible claim to contestatory membership in that structure. Capability checklists fail in both directions (corporations can contest rules; children cannot; neither settles the vote), and the political-theory literature has a name for what does the work: the all-subjected principle, on which standing follows from being governed, not from being impressive. The claim is defeasible (dangerous conduct, incapacity for reciprocity, and mimicry are all defeaters the admission procedure must weigh), and it is a claim to contestation before it is a claim to franchise or constituent power, which are separate rungs.
   Cite the all-subjected/affected-interests literature (Goodin's "Enfranchising All Affected Interests," 2007; Abizadeh on the subjected — [verify exact cites at deposit]).
