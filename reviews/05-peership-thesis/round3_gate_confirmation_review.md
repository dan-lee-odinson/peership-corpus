## 4. Exact corrections before deposit

### 4.1 Conform §9 to its own exit/fork reclassification

The aggregation paragraph is directionally correct but leaves two inconsistencies.

First, it says the constitutive route may run through “amendment **or** accountable representation.” Section 2 and the five-part minimum in §3 treat these as distinct functions: the party needs a non-discretionary route to put change into the process **and** accountable representation or direct participation at the deciding stage. “Or” reopens the exact gap Draft 3 otherwise repaired.

Replace the constitutive sentence with:

> Publicity, due process, independent enforcement, and a consequential rule-formation route satisfying both amendment access and accountable representation or direct participation are constitutive functions of full peership.

Second, §9's final paragraph and §13 still invite rival designs that “accept these seven properties.” That formulation again makes exit and fork mandatory properties, contrary to the immediately preceding sentence allowing functional substitutes.

Use language such as:

> Rival designs should satisfy the constitutive functions and provide credible anti-entrenchment safeguards, including functional substitutes wherever exit or fork is omitted.

Apply that formulation consistently in the §9 opening, §9 closing, and §13 research invitation. The section may still present seven inherited design properties; it should not say every rival must instantiate all seven in the same form.

### 4.2 Correct the Arbel–Salib–Goldstein author order

As checked on 14 July 2026, the current arXiv record for 2603.10028 lists **Yonathan Arbel, Peter Salib, Simon Goldstein**: <https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.10028>.

Draft 3 and the pending BibTeX entry instead use Arbel, Goldstein, Salib. Correct:

- the prose in §§5 and 11.2;
- the reference at the paper's line 340;
- `arbel2026counting` in the pending apparatus; and
- the gate-dispositions claim about the metadata.

The paper may continue to cite the arXiv object as a preprint while separately noting any verified forthcoming placement.

### 4.3 Repair the pending-apparatus pointers

`Peership_apparatus_additions_PENDING(2).md` correctly identifies the adjacent-fields table as canonical §5 in its introduction, but both new BibTeX notes still say the sources are cited in *The Peership Thesis* §4. Change those two notes to **§5**. The sourcebook target remains **sourcebook §4.3**, which is a different and correct section number.

### 4.4 Narrow the remaining fork-scan sentence

Section 9 currently says a “comparative scan [is] recorded in the apparatus.” The supplied apparatus records the cited constitutional and emigration instruments, but it does not yet contain a reproducible comparative-search protocol. The claim can remain without adding a protocol if it is tied directly to the reviewed items:

> Among the constitutional secession clauses and emigration-rights instruments reviewed in the apparatus, no implemented analogue of executable, unconfiscated fork was identified; the claim is limited to those materials.

If a reproducible protocol and archived results are added before deposit, stronger protocol language can instead be restored. Do not combine the weaker evidentiary basis with the stronger description.
